Mapped Operational Rating™

Client Terms & Data Policy

Version 1.0 · Last updated 11 July 2026

1. What the Mapped Operational Rating is

The Mapped Operational Rating (the "Rating") is an independent, evidence-based assessment of how ready your organisation is to operate and scale beyond individual effort. It is a professional opinion, based on the information and evidence you provide. The Rating is not a guarantee of any business outcome, a credit rating or any regulated financial rating, investment, financial, legal or tax advice, a financial audit, or a promise of funding, valuation, sale or growth.

2. Your responsibilities

The Rating reflects the information and evidence you submit. You agree to provide information that is accurate, current and complete. Incomplete or inaccurate inputs will affect both your score and your evidence-confidence level. We may ask clarifying questions or request additional evidence.

3. Sharing your Rating, and third-party reliance

The Rating is prepared for your own use. You may choose to share it with others (for example investors, lenders, partners or your board). However, the Rating is not prepared for the benefit of any third party, and Mapped accepts no responsibility or liability to any third party who relies on it, unless Mapped has agreed to that reliance in writing.

4. Our other services, and how to read our recommendations

Mapped also provides advisory and implementation services, including work to close the gaps a Rating identifies. You should take this into account when reading our recommendations. The roadmap in your Rating is yours to act on, with us or without us, and we will say so plainly.

5. Limitation of liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Mapped's total liability arising from or connected to the Rating is limited to the fee you paid for it. Mapped is not liable for any indirect or consequential loss, or for any decision made by you or anyone else on the basis of the Rating.

6. Confidentiality

Mapped treats the information you provide as confidential. Mapped will not disclose identifiable information about your organisation to third parties without your written permission, except where required by law.

7. Data protection and privacy

Mapped processes your information in line with the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019.

  • What we collect: the information and documents you submit for the assessment, and basic contact details.
  • Why: to produce your Rating and, where relevant, to support you afterwards.
  • Lawful basis: your consent, and the contract to provide the Rating.
  • Storage and security: held in access-controlled storage, seen only by those producing your Rating.
  • Retention: kept for 12 months after your Rating is issued and then deleted, or deleted earlier on request, unless we are required by law to keep it.
  • Your rights: you may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your data, and withdraw consent at any time.
  • Benchmarking: your data may contribute to Mapped's benchmarks only in anonymised and aggregated form, from which your organisation cannot be identified.
  • Data requests: mappedafricameroeafrica@gmail.com.

8. Intellectual property

Mapped owns the Mapped Operational Rating methodology, scoring logic, dimensions, bands, report format and associated marks (including Mapped Operational Rating™ and Mapped Score™). You own the information you submit and the Rating issued to you, for your own use. Mapped may use de-identified, aggregated data to develop its benchmarks and methodology.

9. Payment, refunds and re-review

The fee is KES 13,000 (or USD 100 equivalent). Because the Rating is an independent opinion, a result you did not expect or hope for is not, by itself, grounds for a refund. If you believe your Rating contains a factual error, you may request a re-review within 14 days; we will review the evidence and correct the Rating if warranted. If we are unable to deliver your Rating, your fee will be refunded in full.

10. Re-rating

Your Rating reflects your organisation at the assessment date and is valid for 12 months. You may commission a re-rating at any time to track your progress; a separate fee applies.

11. Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of Kenya. The parties will seek to resolve any dispute amicably; failing that, disputes will be subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Kenya.
