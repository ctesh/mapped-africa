Methodology

How the Mapped Operational Rating works.

We publish our logic on purpose. A rating is only worth carrying if you can see how it was reached. Here is exactly how yours is produced.

Step 1 — What we measure

Seven dimensions of organisational maturity.

Every rating assesses the same seven dimensions — the parts of an organisation that determine whether it can scale beyond the people who built it.

1
Intent Clarity  — knows what it is building, why, and what matters now.
2
Role & Accountability Clarity  — who owns what, who decides.
3
Process Visibility  — how work actually happens.
4
Knowledge Transferability  — knowledge survives beyond individuals.
5
Decision Quality  — decisions are clear, fast, owned, traceable.
6
Systems & Data Readiness  — reliable tools, data and reporting.
7
Learning & Adaptability  — the organisation improves itself on a rhythm.
Step 2 — How each dimension is scored

Declared, evidenced, and coherent.

Each dimension blends three views. Evidence carries the most weight — that is what makes this a rating, not a survey.

30%
DeclaredWhat you tell us in your answers.
50%
EvidenceWhat your documents and data actually prove.
20%
CoherenceHow well the story, evidence and reality align.
Dimension score  =  Declared ×0.30  +  Evidence ×0.50  +  Coherence ×0.20

Each rating is produced and verified by a Mapped assessor against a fixed rubric — not generated automatically. The overall Mapped Score (0–100) is the average of the seven dimension scores.

Step 3 — The band

Your score places you on one of five bands.

Founder-Memory Driven
0–39
Works because key people remember how it works.
Emerging System
40–59
Some structure, but still uneven and informal.
Scaling Foundation
60–74
Enough to grow — specific gaps to close first.
Investment-Grade Operations
75–89
Visible systems; scales beyond individual effort.
Institution-Ready
90–100
Mature, transferable, measurable, resilient.

The ready-to-scale passmark is 70. Investment-Grade Operations begins at 75.

Step 4 — Confidence, flag & outlook

Every rating shows how it was reached.

Evidence confidence

Based on how many of the seven dimensions were backed by real evidence: none → Bronze, some → Silver, most or all → Gold, and Gold with a repeat assessment → Platinum.

Founder-dependency signal

Drawn from whether the business could run without a key person: untested → High, short-absence-only → Medium, tested → Low. Surfaced on its own because it is the headline risk to scale.

Rating outlook

Positive if a clear 90-day path lifts you through the passmark or up a band; Stable if you are past it and holding; Negative where dependency is high and several dimensions sit low.

Your roadmap

The three dimensions furthest below the passmark become your prioritised moves. Closing a gap of X points adds X ÷ 7 to your overall — so the projected climb is a straight recalculation, not a guess.

What we don't claim

The honest limits.

  • — A higher rating does not guarantee better business performance. It indicates stronger operational visibility and readiness, based on the evidence reviewed.
  • — Benchmark and percentile figures become live only once enough verified assessments exist. Until then, they are shown as pending — never invented.
  • — Specific scoring weights are held, but the dimensions, bands and logic on this page are the whole method. Transparency is the point.
