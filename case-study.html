Flagship Case Study

From ten years in the founder's head to an operating model.

How Mapped took a Nairobi design-and-build studio from a business that ran on memory to a redesigned operating model it can train, delegate, and scale on.

Sector
Design and build
Where
Nairobi, Kenya
Situation
10+ years in-house, new model
Engagement
Operating-model redesign

The situation

A respected studio had spent more than a decade delivering interior fitouts the way most founder-led firms do: brilliantly, but in people's heads. As the founder moved the business toward a new, productized model, the informal way of working became the ceiling. The design process depended on the founder. Handoffs between design, sourcing, and build were undocumented. There was no single standard for how a project actually ran. Growth was adding chaos, not control.

What we did

This was not a poster. It was a full operating-model redesign, run over several weeks, end to end.

01 · Discover

Mapped the business as it really runs

We captured the current-state journeys across enquiry, design, sourcing, build, and handover, including the upstream and downstream dependencies most maps miss.

02 · Diagnose

Scored the operating maturity

The Mapped Score diagnostic surfaced the real constraints: founder dependency, undocumented handoffs, and no repeatable standard for the core design process.

03 · Redesign

Designed the To-Be operating model

This is the heart of the work. We designed the ideal-state journeys, the normal design process, the full-service workflow, and onboarding, as a repeatable system with clear roles, decision gates, and dependencies.

04 · Align

Made the team own it, not just receive it

We ran walkthroughs and a structured change-request process so the new model was pressure-tested and adopted, not handed over and shelved.

05 · Embed

Handed over a phased path to keep improving

We closed the engagement cleanly and set up a continuous-improvement phase, so the studio keeps refining the model as the business evolves.

Before and after

⚑ Current state

  • The design process lived in the founder's head
  • Handoffs between design, sourcing, and build were undocumented
  • No single standard for how a project ran
  • New hires learned by shadowing, slowly
  • Growth created more firefighting

✓ To-be operating model

  • Ideal-state journeys designed for the core processes
  • Roles, decision gates, and dependencies made explicit
  • A repeatable full-service workflow and onboarding path
  • The model documented so it can be trained and delegated
  • A phased roadmap for continuous improvement
The business moved from running on memory to running on a system, one it can teach, delegate, and scale, instead of one that lives and dies with the founder.

Why it matters

Most process work stops at the diagnosis, the pretty map with the problems circled. The value is in the redesign: the to-be operating model, the dependency logic, and the change work that gets a team to actually adopt it. That is the real Mapped engagement, and it is what turns a founder-led business into an asset that can be funded, delegated, and automated.
